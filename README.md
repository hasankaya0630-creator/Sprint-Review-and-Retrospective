# Sprint-Review-and-Retrospective

How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating user stories help with this?

I interpret user needs by identifying what the user wants to accomplish and translating those goals into specific features and requirements. Creating user stories helps break larger projects into smaller, manageable tasks while keeping the focus on the user's perspective. This makes it easier to prioritize work and verify that the final product meets the user's expectations.

How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?

I approach development by breaking projects into smaller tasks, planning each step, and testing as I build. Throughout this course, I learned the value of Agile practices such as sprint planning, regular reviews, and retrospectives. In future projects, I plan to continue using iterative development, frequent testing, and continuous feedback to improve both the quality of my work and my ability to adapt to changing requirements.

 What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?

Being a good team member means communicating clearly, completing assigned work on time, and supporting other team members when needed. It also means being open to feedback, participating in discussions, and working toward shared goals. Agile development relies on collaboration, so contributing positively to the team is just as important as writing quality code.
